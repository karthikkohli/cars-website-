TOP 3 BEST SELLING CARS

1.TATA NEXON

The Tata Nexon is a subcompact crossover SUV produced by the Indian automaker Tata Motors since 2017. It is the first crossover SUV from the brand and occupies the sub-4 meter crossover SUV segment in India. The Nexon made its debut as a prototype exhibited at the Auto Expo 2014.[3] The final model was presented in February 2016. The Nexon is based on the revised Tata X1 platform that debuted in 1998 with the Indica model and was adopted by other Indian brand cars.[4] It uses an independent MacPherson dual-path strut with coil spring front suspensions and rear twist-beam with coil spring and shock absorber. The wheelbase is measured at 2,498 mm (98.3 in). It is characterized by two-tone paint (optional), dual halogen headlamps with projector low beams, and large chrome bands along the side.

2.MARUTI SUZUKI DZIRE

The Suzuki Dzire (stylized DZire, previously knows as the Suzuki Swift Dzire), known as the Suzuki Swift Sedan in Colombia and Guatemala, [1][2] is a subcompact notchback sedan made by Suzuki mainly for the Indian market since 2008. It was developed as a sedan variation of the Swift hatchback. The Dzire was launched in 2008 and was based on the first generation Swift hatchback. It was introduced as a subcompact sedan for the Indian market in order to offer a sedan-like car in a smaller size. Later, in order to get the tax benefits offered to cars with a length below 4 meters in India, it was converted into a sub-4-meter sedan

3.TATA PUNCH

The Tata Punch is a crossover city car (A-segment) manufactured by Tata Motors Cars since 2021. Positioned as the smallest SUV of the brand below the Nexon,[1] the Punch is built on ALFA-ARC platform shared with the Altroz hatchback.The concept was revealed as H2X compact SUV (codenamed Hornbill) at the 89th Geneva International Motor Show in 2019,[4] followed by the near-production HBX concept at the Auto Expo 2020.[5][2] On 23 August 2021, the vehicle was revealed with the name Punch. The car was unveiled on 4 October 2021.[6] It is powered by a 1.2-litre Revotron three-cylinder petrol engine which shared with the Altroz, Tiago, and Tigor.
